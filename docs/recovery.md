# Recovery and Retirement

DID One follows the Recover or Retire law.

```text
Every failing thing must either recover or retire.
No permanent limbo.
No silent broken state.
No zombie agents.
No orphan systems.
```

## Recovery law

```text
Failure does not mean death.
Concern does not mean freeze.
Residue does not surface as truth.
Repair must be possible.
Exit must be graceful.
History must remain verifiable.
```

## Recovery states

```text
0 healthy
1 concern_detected
2 degraded
3 constrained
4 quarantined
5 repair_started
6 repaired
7 verification_pending
8 verified
9 restored
10 retired
11 archived
12 unrecoverable
```

## Decision

Recover if trust, proof, and compatibility can be rebuilt.

Retire if trust is broken beyond repair, identity is compromised, recovery cost exceeds value, compatibility cannot be preserved, or repeated failure creates systemic risk.
